      <h2 class="pane-title">Welcome to Penn Chemistry</h2>
  
  
  <div class="pane-content">
    <p>Penn Chemistry is composed of a dynamic community of researchers creating and disseminating new knowledge at the forefront of the chemical sciences. As an enabling science, chemistry is at the focal point of every important modern societal challenge. Our faculty and students engage these challenges daily on a local, national, and international scale.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://twitter.com/PennChemistry"><img src="/sites/www.chem.upenn.edu/files/uploads/twitter_32.png" alt="Twitter" width="32" height="32" /></a>&nbsp;<a href="http://www.linkedin.com/groups?mostPopular=&amp;gid=2254948"><img src="/sites/www.chem.upenn.edu/files/uploads/linkedin_32.png" alt="LinkedIn" width="32" height="32" /></a>&nbsp;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/61311255924/"><img src="/sites/www.chem.upenn.edu/files/uploads/facebook_32.png" alt="Facebook" width="32" height="32" /></a></p>  </div>

<div class="panel-region-separator"></div><div class="panel-pane pane-block pane-block-3" >
  
      <h2 class="pane-title">Research Highlight</h2>
  
  
  <div class="pane-content">
    <div>
<div>
<div>
<div>
<div><img src="/sites/www.chem.upenn.edu/files/uploads/saven.jpg" alt="Saven" width="250" height="350" /></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Jeff Saven is one of several Penn&nbsp;researchers leading an effort to create an artificial chemical sensor that is critical in the action of painkillers and anesthetics. Receptors in these sensors produce an electrical response that can then be read out by a computer. This research shows a way to mass produce devices that could be useful in drug development and a variety of diagnostic tests.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This researdh effort is a University-wide project made possible through the Penn Nano/Bio Interface Center (NBIC), which brings together investigators from the Schools of Engineering and Applied Science; Arts and Sciences; and Medicine. Read more about the project <a href="http://www.upenn.edu/pennnews/news/penn-research-combines-graphene-and-painkiller-receptor-scalable-chemical-sensor">here</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>  </div>
